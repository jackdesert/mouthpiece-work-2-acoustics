SUBJECT: Re: Putting a Curve on that facing
A facing length of 40 mm is way too long for tenor sax.

Facing length, as measured with the .0015" feeler ("one and a half thousandths") should be 
about 24 mm for a medium length facing (a "48" on most glass gauges).

For most facings, the next feeler should be the .010" and be 5 mm shorter, or 19 mm (38 on 
the glass gauge).

The .015" (I use a .014", but it won't make any difference) will be another 2mm shorter, or 
17 mm (34 on the glass gauge).

Your .002" through .008" feelers are not really useful other than perhaps substituting the 
.002" for the .0015" feeler in order to use a little more durable feeler.  It will give practically 
the same reading as the .0015".  The same is true of the .008" and .010".  They are so 
close as to not be useful.

You need also a .024" or .025" feeler, .031" (some use .034" or .035"), .048" (or .050"), 
.063", .078", .094".  These will work for most clarinet, soprano sax, alto, tenor and baritone 
sax mouthpieces.  You might also want .108" and .120" for working on larger tenor and 
bari mouthpieces.

Make sure of your glass guage.  Is it marked in mm's or in half mm's?  Use a ruler to 
check.  Does 10 on the gauge measure as 10 mm or 5 mm?

Paul C.

Soprano 16-17 (medium), 18 mm (long)

SUBJECT: Re: Putting a Curve on that facing
I do not get accurate measurements with "stacked" feelers.  Try to get the correct 
feelers, or ones close.  For example, a .0015" or .002", either will work fine for 
facing length, with the .002" being more durable but measuring perhaps 0.5 - 1 mm 
shorter.  Not a big deal is you are aware of it. (metric equivalents 0,04 and 0,05 mm)

For the seconds feeler, .010"  (or metric equivilent 0,25mm).

For the third feeler, .014" or .015".  (0.35 mm or 0,4 mm)  These will give almost 
identical measurements.

For the fourth feeler, .024" or .025". (0,6 mm)  These will give almost identical 
measurements.

.048" - .050" (1.2 mm)  These will give almost identical measurements.

.063"  (1.6 mm)

.077" or .078" (2.0 mm)

.094"  (2.4 mm)  You could probably also use 2.5 mm, but 2.5 mm (.098") is a 
popular tip opening for tenor, and you could not the the feeler measurement just 
short of the tip.

SUBJECT: Re: Putting a Curve on that facing
One suggestion is to use a pencil to mark the rails of the mouthpiece.  You could mark the rails as an example at your first station  ie. 50.  The .0015 spot.  Once you have this measurement happening then use the pencil to show this is done.  You can mark the area of 50 and more on the rails.   As you work on the next station say. .010 make sure you don't remove any pencil marks or you are undoing your work.  Devise your own system.  In my experience I have found that obtaining the feel for what part of the curve you are working on exactly is difficult.   The pencil helps.  
I have also dabbled with using rollers as a guide getting a curve.  Will Grizzle suggested it and said it came from Johnston.   You can use drill bits of certain diameters (non cutting portion). and place one under the table of the mouthpiece.  Roll it back and forth.  I used maybe 5 or 6 of these and some needle springs for a few stations furthest from the tip.
